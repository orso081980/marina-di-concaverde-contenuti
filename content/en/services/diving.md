Between **Santa Teresa Gallura** and **Palau** there are numerous authorised diving schools and centres to take you exploring the most beautiful and life-filled seabeds — rivalling coral reefs in the most exotic destinations.

Whether a half-day trip or a full course to get your diving certificate or improve your skills.

## Gallura Seabeds

The waters of the Strait of Bonifacio and the La Maddalena Archipelago hide an underwater world of extraordinary beauty:

- **Posidonia meadows** (_Posidonia oceanica_): the symbolic plant of the Mediterranean, indicator of clean waters, is abundant along the Porto Pozzo coast
- **Granite caves and crevices**: pink granite rocks create underwater labyrinths inhabited by groupers, moray eels and octopuses
- **Rich fauna**: large groupers, barracuda, octopuses, sea urchins, lobsters, starfish, nudibranchs
- **Coralligeno meadows**: among the most biodiverse sites in the Mediterranean
- **Secca del Papa** (near Palau): vertical wall with red sea fans
- **Spargi Wreck**: Roman shipwreck from the 1st century BC, archaeological site of interest
