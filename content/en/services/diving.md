Between **Santa Teresa Gallura** and **Palau** there are numerous authorized diving schools and centers to take you to explore the most beautiful and life-filled seabeds.

Whether it's a half-day outing or a full course to get your certification or improve your knowledge.

## Gallura's Seabeds

The waters of the Bonifacio Strait and the La Maddalena archipelago hide an underwater world of extraordinary beauty:

- **Seagrass meadows** (_Posidonia oceanica_): the plant symbol of the Mediterranean, an indicator of clean waters, is abundant along the Porto Pozzo coast
- **Granite caves and crevices**: pink granite rocks create underwater labyrinths inhabited by groupers, moray eels and octopuses
- **Rich fauna**: large groupers, barracuda, octopuses, sea urchins, lobsters, starfish, nudibranchs
- **Coralline meadows**: among the richest biodiversity sites in the Mediterranean
- **Papa Reef** (near Palau): vertical wall with red gorgonians
- **Spargi Wreck**: Roman shipwreck from 1st century BC, archaeological site of interest
